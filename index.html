MOUNT.FISHTAIL

Mount Fishtail, also known as Machapuchare, is a prominent peak in the Annapurna Himalayas of north-central Nepal. Standing at 6,993 meters (22,943 feet), it is renowned for its distinctive twin summits, which resemble a fish's tail, giving it its name. Machapuchare is considered sacred by the local Gurung people and is associated with the Hindu god Shiva. Consequently, climbing the peak is prohibited, preserving its pristine condition. The mountain's striking appearance and its significance in local culture make it a notable landmark in the region, attracting trekkers and photographers who admire it from nearby trails like the Annapurna Base Camp trek.
